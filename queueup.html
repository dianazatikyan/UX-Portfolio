QueueUp App

Let's Get Started

User Research

We surveyed 25 participants and interviewed 5 to understand what our users expect from a perfect dining experience and identify major pain points. Our objectives were:

1

Identify the pain points of choosing a restaurant.

2

Learn how to improve the experience of searching for places to eat.

3

Understand how much of a priority wait time is for the user.

User 1

“The bitter taste of how long it took to get to eat our food just ruined the whole dining experience.”

User 2

"When I spend money, I want to know I am going to like it. I am ok to wait for my favorite food."

User 3

"If I’m dinning with a group, I want to impress them with a good pick. Wait time doesn’t matter as much."

User 4

"Wait time kills it for me at times, but can’t avoid wait time for good food."

The Problem

Identifying the Pain Point

While conducting user interviews, we identified a common pain point. Users didn't mind waiting in line for their preferred restaurant, but they struggled with deciding how to spend their time while waiting, particularly when in larger groups.

This waiting period could disrupt the lively ambiance of the evening.

Let's Get Visual

Storyboard

To ensure a seamless user journey, we designed a simple and fluid process. Users only needed to input the restaurant name and estimated wait time, and they would receive a list of nearby bars, parks, and attractions. The app would track their time and factor in distance, and users would receive a timer and a reminder to leave for the restaurant when the time is almost up.

Various Iterations

Prototyping

As UI Design Lead, my responsibility was to merge the low-fidelity prototypes from my team members into a high-fidelity product. I aimed to create a sleek and contemporary appearance for the app, incorporating a bold primary color, sans-serif typography, and rounded buttons.

Finish Line

Final Prototype

My objective was to provide users with a streamlined onboarding experience that required minimal clicks. To ensure users wouldn't miss their spot in line, I suggested incorporating a live timer and progress bar at the top of the screen. Analyzing similar review-based booking apps, such as Open Table and Yelp, I designed the home page to feel intuitive for users.

With QueueUp, waiting in line is a thing of the past.
