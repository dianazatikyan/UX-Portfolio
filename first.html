User Research

Our team interviewed 6 university alumni about their participation in donations to their alma mater. We also had them perform 3 simple tasks on the current website.

Analysis

After user interviews and tests, we focused on dissecting our research through affinity diagrams, empathy map and a prioritization matrix.

Design

We focused on incorporating the UC Berkeley aesthetic into the website to make the experience of navigating to the website more seamless and trustworthy.

Testing

The prototypes were tested a total of 9 times with new iterations created after each test. We adjusted every detail which seemed off to our users.

Empathy Phase

How does the user feel?

After conducting user interviews and user tests using the current site, our team was able to hone in on what the users prioritized most. Many felt they wanted to be reassured the organization can be trusted and is connected to UC Berkeley.

Many users wanted to see where exactly their donation will go and how it makes an impact. Another theme which came up frequently was having quick access to the mission statement to clear up any concerns.

Getting Organized

Sitemapping

In order to focus on facilitating the navigation process, we restructured the information architecture through card sorting.
